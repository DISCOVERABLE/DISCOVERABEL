Payment Information

On DISCOVERABLE™ you can be paid for programming.
There are different types of payment methods so everyone can be fairly paid for the work they do. This allows fixable working for both the employer and employee.

No Payment

For this type of work you will not get paid. This work is for fun and when the code is submitted is no longer yours to own. This is just for small projects and people who like to code for fun.

Per Hour

For this type of work you will will be paid. The amount you get paid will depend on the amount of time you work. The more you work the more you will get paid there may be a cap imposed e.g. Your being paid £12.97 an hour and you work for 100 hours you will be paid £1297.00, the employer can impose a cap such the cap may be £950 and this is what you will be eligible to be paid, as always the employer can pay the full £1297.00 if they are particularly pleased with the work carried out. It is up to the employee to prove and keep track of the hours worked. If the employee or employer feels that there is been unfair treatment they can raise the issue with us here.

One Time Payment

One time payment allows the employee to get paid for a single job they do. This may include writing web page, a piece of code or designing something. The amount that the employee will get paid is specified before the job is carried out. If the employer is unhappy with the work carried out they can refuse to pay; as always if the employee feels unfairly treated they can raise the issue with us here.

Per Line Of Code

This option will allow the employee to e paid. The amount they get paid is based on how many lines of code are completed Bank lines will not be counted neither will comments. e.g. The employee codes 1200 lines of code of these 1200, 97 are blank or comments. This will leave the employee having coded 1103, the pre determined rate was £0.005 per line this means the employee will be paid £5.515 the amount will be rounded to the nearest pence so the employee will be paid £5.52. If the employer is unhappy with the work carried out they can refuse to pay; as always if the employee feels unfairly treated they can raise the issue with us here.

Per Job/Task Completed

Being paid per job or task is very similar to a one off payment. The employer will set a list of jobs or tasks for the employee to do and for each task completed they will be paid. Different jobs and tasks can be set to be paid at different amounts such that a small task will be lower paid than a larger more complex task. If the employer is unhappy with the work carried out they can refuse to pay; as always if the employee feels unfairly treated they can raise the issue with us here.

Voluntary Payment

The employer has no obligation to pay for any of the work done. If the employer feels that the work carried out if expectantly good a sum may be paid, the amount paid is completely up to the employer.

Price On Application

The amount paid for this job will be disclosed when the employee is accepted for the job. The amount and payment type may be discussed in a bilateral discussion between both parties, employee and employer, to determine how-much and what payment type there will be for the work. If the employer is unhappy with the work carried out they can refuse to pay; as always if the employee feels unfairly treated they can raise the issue with us here.
